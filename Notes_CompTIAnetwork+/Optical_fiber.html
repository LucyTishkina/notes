Optical fiber


Fiber communication
- transmission by light
→ the visible spectrum
- No RF signal
→ very difficult to monitor or tap
- Signal slow to degrade
→ Transmission over long distances
- Immune to radio interference
→ There's no RF

- Core coverd by cladding with lower index of refraction causing light to reflect off the cladding staying in the core
- Coating around to protect the cable
- The end is covered by a ceramic ferrule to allow for connection/disconnection of cable without damaging the fiber

Multimode-fiber
- short-range communication
→ up to 2km
- inexpensive light source
→ i.e. LED
- Core of the fiber is larger than the wavelength of light going through

Single-mode fiber
- Long-range communication
→ Up to 100 km without process
- Expensive light source
→ laser beams
- narrow core

UPC vs APC
- controlling light
→ laws of physics apply
- return loss
→ light reflected back to the source
- UPC (Ultra-polished connectors)
→ Ferrule end-face radius polished at a zero degree angle
→ high return loss
- APC (Angle-polished connectors)
→ ferrule end-face radius polished at an eight degree angle
- Lower return loss, generally higher insertion loss than UPC
